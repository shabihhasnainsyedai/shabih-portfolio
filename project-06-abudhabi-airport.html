Overview

The Abu Dhabi International Airport Expansion was one of the largest and most complex infrastructure programs in the Gulf region — a multi-billion dollar, decade-long initiative to dramatically expand the capacity of Abu Dhabi's primary international gateway. PARSONS International served as Program Management Consultant with a team of 150–200 professionals coordinating across dozens of contractors, consultants, and government stakeholders.

At this scale, cost, schedule, contract, and reporting management had to be systematic, accurate, and capable of generating executive-grade outputs monthly. Prior to the systems implementation, this entire function was manual — consuming enormous effort and introducing unacceptable risk of error on a publicly critical program.

Challenge & Opportunity
⚠ Challenge

With 150–200 program professionals, multiple contractor interfaces, and a 10+ year timeline, executive reporting was done entirely by hand. Monthly cost and schedule reports required dozens of hours of data collection, consolidation, and formatting — creating a recurring bottleneck and exposing the program to data accuracy risks at the leadership level.

✦ Opportunity

ARES-PRISM and PRISM G2, combined with Primavera P6, provided a proven enterprise program controls platform capable of automating cost actuals, commitments, contract variations, and baseline tracking — replacing the manual reporting cycle with a system-driven process that could scale with the program.

Solution Delivery
1

ARES-PRISM & PRISM G2 Implementation

Implemented and fully configured ARES-PRISM and PRISM G2 for the complete program controls function — covering Contracts Management, Variations (change orders), commitments tracking, and Primavera P6 schedule integration.

2

Monthly Actuals & Commitment Import Cycle

Configured and managed the monthly cycle of actual cost imports, commitment updates, and baseline estimate tracking within PRISM — providing the program team with an always-current view of cost performance against the approved budget.

3

Primavera P6 Schedule Integration

Maintained the integration between PRISM and Primavera P6 to keep schedule and cost data aligned — enabling earned value analysis and a unified view of program performance across both time and cost dimensions.

4

Executive Reporting Automation

Replaced the manual monthly executive reporting process with system-generated PRISM reports and dashboards — eliminating 60–80 hours per month of manual data collection and delivering more accurate, consistent outputs to leadership.

5

Contracts & Variations Management

Configured the Contracts and Variations modules to track all contract awards, change events, and variation orders across the program's multiple contractor interfaces — full visibility of contractual commitments and budget impact.
