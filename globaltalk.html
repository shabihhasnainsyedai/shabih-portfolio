"I asked it for Pashto. It gave me Urdu. Fifteen iterations later, it apologised."

That's the experience that built Global Talk. A real problem, encountered while building in public. The AI kept defaulting to Urdu — a close language, but not the right one.


Global Talk uses a three-step Pashto validation chain: if the output doesn't contain Pashto-specific script characters (ړ ږ ګ ځ), it retries — automatically, silently, until it's right.


That's not a feature. That's the minimum standard for a language that deserves better.
